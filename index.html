How Fast Reading Works

The science: RSVP (Rapid Serial Visual Presentation)

Traditional reading is slowed by saccades — the eye movements your eyes make to jump across a line of text. Each saccade takes 20–200 ms and forces a brief pause. RSVP eliminates saccades entirely by presenting words one at a time in a fixed location. Your eyes stay still; only the words change.

Research shows trained readers can reach 600–1000 WPM with good comprehension using RSVP, compared to a typical 200–300 WPM for conventional reading.

  • Rayner et al. (2016) — critique and limits of speed reading
  • Benedetto et al. (2015) — RSVP comprehension study
  • Schneps et al. — RSVP benefits for readers with dyslexia

Scroll mode

Smooth horizontal scrolling simulates the "moving window" technique. A fixed-width viewport lets your eye anchor on one spot while the text flows past — like a teleprompter. Narrower windows force more focus; wider windows provide more context.

How to use the settings

  • WPM — words per minute. Start at 200–250, work up gradually.
  • Size — font size. Larger is easier on mobile.
  • Flash / Scroll — toggle reading mode.
  • Max words / Max chars — group short words together (e.g. "and he was" at once). Reduces flicker and aids rhythm.
  • Hyphen at — any word longer than this many characters is split with a hyphen across two flashes.
  • Window (scroll) — visible character width in scroll mode. 30–50 ch is a comfortable starting point.
  • Scrub bar — drag to jump to any point in the text; surrounding context appears while dragging.

Tips

  • Start at 200 WPM and increase by 20–50 WPM per session.
  • Use grouping (max words 2–3) to reduce cognitive load at higher speeds.
  • Try scroll mode for narrative fiction — it feels more natural.
  • Comprehension drops sharply above 600 WPM for most people — prioritize understanding over raw speed.
