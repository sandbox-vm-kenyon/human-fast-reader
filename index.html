Complexity slowdown

What it does

  • Bigger / busier chunk → longer display time.
  • Adds to (not multiplies with) the punctuation pacing pause.

Multiplier

  • Slowdown reaches this value at ~10 effective chars.
  • 1.00 = off.

Exponent (curve shape)

  • = 1 linear.
  • > 1 curves up — outliers feel it more.
  • < 1 curves down — fast early, then plateaus.

Invert curve

  • Mirrors the curve about y = x: y = xz → y = x1/z.
  • Same shape, swapped axes — exponent 2 inverted = square-root growth.

Effective length

  • Characters + (extra-words bonus, when toggled on).
  • Hyphens count as chars and as word splitters.

Try these

  • Default — 1.20 × 1.5 · slight curve up.
  • Linear — 1.80 × 1.0 · proportional.
  • Front-loaded — 1.50 × 2.0, invert ON · square-root growth; fast early rise, keeps climbing slowly past 10.
  • Outlier-focused — 1.20 × 2.5 · short chunks untouched, long ones heavily penalized.

RSVP speed reading claims are mostly false!

RSVP, or Rapid Serial Visual Presentation, has gotten recent attention because of speed-reading claims that are unproven in real-world application where retention is actually measured.

HOWEVER — RSVP enhancements have shown promise for speed and retention

Research indicates that RSVP performance may improve (performance trends positive) when pacing and flow are enhanced. This app explores RSVP configurations associated with those measured improvements to enhance and adjust flow and phrasing.

RSVP does have proven benefits for people with visual impairments or ADHD

Certain vision impairments have been shown to benefit from RSVP-style reading, and research has been done showing benefits in focus for people with ADHD.

Links:

  • RSVP caution / speed-reading limits: Acklin & Papesh (2017)
  • ADHD / focus-related RSVP benefit: Cambridge — Reading without eye movements (ADHD)
  • Visual limitation / peripheral reading training: ScienceDirect — Vision Research
  • Phrase/sentence boundary pacing pilot: Ferguson (Auburn, 2024)

Speed (RSVP) mode

One chunk at a time in a fixed spot. Optionally show leading and trailing words for peripheral context without breaking the RSVP effect. Text is selectable.

Page mode

Shows flowing text with your current reading position highlighted and original line breaks preserved. Switches bidirectionally with Speed mode — position stays in sync. Use Page mode to skip past title pages or a table of contents, then switch back to Speed to resume from that exact word.

Reading Mode & Color Scheme

Speed / Page — switch reading mode. Dark / Light — switch color theme (black/orange or blue/white).

Playback controls

|< 1st   << 10   < 1   ▶ Play   1 >   10 >>   last >| — navigate by chunk, 10 chunks, or jump to start/end.

WPM buttons: <<40   <10   300 WPM   10>   40>>

Settings

  • Size — RSVP display font size (0.5–5 rem).
  • WPM — chunk rate; displayed WPM accounts for word grouping and splitting.
  • Simple / Advanced / Master — presets: 1w/10c, 3w/10c, 7w/20c hyphen thresholds.
  • Max words / Max chars — group short words into one chunk (e.g. "and he was").
  • Hyphen at — hard-split very long strings across chunks.
  • Split hyphenated words above char limit — splits naturally hyphenated words (e.g. "self-aware") at the hyphen before applying the hard-split rule. Supports all Unicode hyphen/dash characters.
  • Show leading & trailing words — context words either side of the current chunk.
  • Scrub bar — drag to jump anywhere; context preview appears while dragging.

Built-in books

10 public domain classics (Project Gutenberg), each loaded at the first word of actual book content, skipping the preamble automatically.

You can also load any .pdf, .txt, .md, or other text file from your device, from a URL, or paste text directly.

Tips

  • Start at 200–250 WPM and increase gradually per session.
  • Use Page mode to skip past front matter, then switch back to Speed.
  • Use grouping (Simple/Advanced profiles) to reduce cognitive load at higher speeds.
  • Comprehension drops sharply above 600 WPM for most people.

Keyboard shortcuts

  • Space — play / pause (Speed mode)
  • ← — back 10 chunks
  • → — forward 1 chunk / next page
  • ↑ / ↓ — speed +50 / −50 WPM
